Pastries: The Art of Layers

Pastries are baked goods made with ingredients such as flour, sugar, milk, butter, shortening, baking powder, and eggs. The key to great pastries lies in the technique of creating layers.

Types of Pastries:

Puff Pastry

Made with layers of butter and dough, creates a light, flaky texture when baked.

Choux Pastry

A light pastry dough used to make √©clairs, profiteroles, and cream puffs.

Shortcrust Pastry

A basic pastry used for pies and tarts, made with flour, fat, and water.

Breads: The Foundation of Baking

Bread is a staple food prepared by baking dough made from flour and water. The process of bread making involves fermentation, which gives bread its distinctive flavor and texture.

Bread Categories:

Yeast Breads

Risen breads that use yeast as a leavening agent, including white bread, whole wheat, and sourdough.

Quick Breads

Breads that use baking powder or soda instead of yeast, like banana bread and muffins.

Flatbreads

Unleavened breads that are rolled flat, such as tortillas, naan, and pita bread.

Cakes: Sweet Celebrations

Cakes are sweet baked goods typically made with flour, sugar, eggs, and butter. They're often decorated with frosting and used for celebrations and special occasions.

Cake Varieties:

Sponge Cakes

Light, airy cakes made with eggs, sugar, and flour. Perfect for layering with cream and fruit.

Butter Cakes

Rich, dense cakes made with butter, including pound cake and yellow cake.

Foam Cakes

Cakes leavened by beaten eggs, such as angel food cake and chiffon cake.
